Carrots

Carrots:

Description: Carrots are root vegetables known for their vibrant orange color and sweet, crunchy taste. They have feathery green tops and grow underground, producing elongated taproots.

Benefits: Carrots are rich in beta-carotene, which the body converts into vitamin A, essential for vision health. They also provide fiber, vitamins, and minerals, supporting digestion and overall well-being.

Steps:
Soil Preparation: Choose a location with loose, well-drained soil free from rocks and debris. Work the soil to a depth of at least 12 inches, breaking up clumps and adding compost for nutrients.

Planting: Sow carrot seeds directly into the prepared soil, spacing them about 2 inches apart and covering them with a thin layer of soil. Keep the soil consistently moist until germination.

Thinning: Once the carrot seedlings have sprouted, thin them to about 2 inches apart to allow room for the roots to develop fully.

Watering: Water carrots regularly, keeping the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged to prevent cracking and encourage steady growth.

Mulching: Apply a layer of mulch around carrot plants to retain moisture, suppress weeds, and protect the roots from temperature fluctuations.

Harvesting: Harvest carrots when they reach the desired size, usually 2-3 months after planting, by gently pulling them from the soil. Rinse off any dirt before consuming.
