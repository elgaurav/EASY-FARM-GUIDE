Bell Peppers

Bell Peppers:

Description: Bell peppers are colorful, bell-shaped vegetables with a sweet flavor. They come in various colors, including green, red, yellow, and orange, and are rich in vitamins C and A.

Benefits: Bell peppers are low in calories and high in nutrients, making them an excellent addition to a healthy diet. They provide antioxidants that support immune function, promote eye health, and reduce inflammation.

Steps: Site Selection: Choose a sunny location with well-drained soil.

Planting: Plant bell pepper seedlings in the garden after the last frost date. Space the plants 18-24 inches apart in rows or raised beds.

Watering: Water the pepper plants regularly, keeping the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged. Mulch around the plants to retain soil moisture and suppress weeds.

Fertilizing: Apply a balanced fertilizer every 4-6 weeks during the growing season to promote healthy growth and fruit production.

Support: Stake or cage the pepper plants to provide support as they grow and produce fruit. Harvesting: Harvest bell peppers when they reach their full size and color. Use pruning shears or scissors to cut the peppers from the plant, leaving a short stem attached.
