Spinach

Spinach:

Description: Spinach is a leafy green vegetable with tender, dark green leaves. It's rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as iron, folate, and antioxidants.

Benefits: Spinach supports overall health due to its nutrient density, offering vitamins and minerals crucial for various bodily functions. It's particularly known for its role in promoting eye health, supporting bone health, and boosting the immune system.

Steps:
Site Selection: Choose a partially shaded spot with fertile, well-draining soil.

Planting: Sow spinach seeds directly into the soil in early spring or late summer. Plant the seeds about Â½ inch deep and 2 inches apart in rows.

Watering: Keep the soil consistently moist by watering regularly, especially during dry spells. Avoid overhead watering to prevent fungal diseases.

Mulching: Apply a layer of organic mulch around the spinach plants to conserve moisture, suppress weeds, and maintain soil temperature.

Fertilizing: Apply a balanced fertilizer every 4-6 weeks to provide essential nutrients for healthy growth.

Harvesting: Harvest spinach leaves when they are young and tender, typically around 6-8 weeks after planting. Use scissors to cut the outer leaves, allowing the inner leaves to continue growing for future harvests.
